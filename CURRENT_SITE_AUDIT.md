# Current Site Audit

## Executive assessment

The repository is a functioning GitHub Pages academic site, but the public-facing identity is materially out of date and weak for current industry/research hiring. The primary problem is not missing content; it is positioning, hierarchy, freshness, and visual presentation.

## Confirmed current-state issues

### Stale identity and contact information

- Homepage and author sidebar describe Jiayi as a second-year PhD student.
- Location is Minneapolis, Minnesota.
- Primary contact is an old University of Minnesota email.
- Homepage copy is written as a graduate-student research profile.
- Footer and public presentation still reflect an older site state.

### Stale resume

- The CV page links to a 2022 PDF.
- The page lists the Ph.D. as “expected 2024.”
- Work experience and skills stop well before current Research Scientist and AI-assisted evaluation work.

### Weak current positioning

- Navigation prioritizes Publications, Talks, Teaching, and CV.
- Portfolio is disabled.
- There is no featured AI fairness or human–AI evaluation project.
- Current applied work, high-impact selection/assessment experience, AI-assisted alignment research, and AI meta-analysis are not visible.

### Generic metadata and SEO

- Site description is a placeholder.
- Title is generic and does not communicate expertise.
- Social/author metadata contains stale or incomplete values.
- Analytics config is obsolete/empty and should not imply tracking is active.

### Visual limitations

- Legacy stock AcademicPages/Minimal Mistakes appearance.
- Gray default palette and modest hierarchy.
- No modern hero, featured-project treatment, proof points, research-theme cards, or strong CTAs.
- The design reads as a template archive rather than a current professional portfolio.

## What should be preserved

- Publication collection and historical research record
- Talks and teaching content
- Google Scholar, ORCID, GitHub, and other verified profile links
- Existing redirects/deep links
- Profile image unless the user provides a newer one
- Jekyll/GitHub Pages architecture for maintainability

## Recommended positioning

`Applied Research Scientist | AI Fairness, Psychometrics & Human-AI Evaluation`

The site should connect four strands:

1. psychometric validity and fairness;
2. high-impact assessment/selection systems;
3. generative-AI-assisted expert workflows and end-to-end evaluation;
4. reproducible research tools and evidence synthesis.

## Key hiring-manager narrative

Jiayi is not merely an education researcher pivoting to AI. She is a measurement scientist with deep experience validating high-impact decision systems, studying subgroup comparability and behavioral evidence, and applying that foundation to generative-AI-assisted workflows and human–AI fairness audits.
